1. Algebra (35% of SAT Math)

Linear equations, systems, inequalities, slopes, and line equations — the largest portion of the SAT.

Solve any Equation
Solves any single-variable equation for x. Shows steps: rearrange → factor → solve → verify.
Input: Equation= 2x+3=7
Practice Question

If 3(x − 2) + 5 = 2x + 7, what is the value of x?

Enter: 3(x-2)+5=2x+7

Answer: x = 8

Solve 2×2 System
Solves a system of two linear equations using substitution and verification.
Inputs: 1.Eqn= x+y=5   2.Eqn= 2x-y=1
Practice Question

A store sells pencils for $0.50 and pens for $1.25. You buy 8 items totaling $6.50. How many of each?

Enter: x+y=8 and 0.5x+1.25y=6.5

Answer: x = 4 pencils, y = 4 pens

Solve 3×3 System
Solves a system of three linear equations in x, y, z.
Inputs: x+y+z=6, 2x-y+z=3, x+2y-z=3
Practice Question

If x + y + z = 10, 2x + y = 7, and y + 3z = 15, find x, y, and z.

Answer: x = 1, y = 5, z = 4

Linear Inequality
Solves linear inequalities in one variable using the CAS engine.
Input: Inequality= 2x+3>7
Practice Question

A phone plan charges $30/month plus $0.10 per text. If you want to spend less than $50, how many texts can you send?

Enter: 30+0.1x<50

Answer: x < 200 texts

Simplify & Evaluate
Expands, factors, simplifies, and evaluates expressions. Also shows derivative and integral.
Input: Expr= (x+3)^2-9
Practice Question

Which of the following is equivalent to (2x + 3)² − 4x²?

Enter: (2x+3)^2-4x^2

Answer: 12x + 9

Find y = mx + b
Given slope m and y-intercept b, shows the line equation and x-intercept.
Inputs: m= 2   b= -3
Practice Question

A line has slope 3 and passes through (0, −5). What is the x-intercept?

Enter: m= 3, b= -5

Answer: x-intercept = (5/3, 0) ≈ (1.667, 0)

Find Slope
Calculates slope, midpoint, distance, and line equation from two points.
Inputs: x1= 1 y1= 2 x2= 4 y2= 8
Practice Question

Line p passes through (−3, 7) and (5, 1). What is the slope of p?

Enter: x1= -3 y1= 7 x2= 5 y2= 1

Answer: m = −3/4

Point-Slope Form
Given a point and slope, produces point-slope and slope-intercept forms.
Inputs: x1= 2 y1= 5 m= 3
Practice Question

Write the equation of the line through (4, −1) with slope −2 in slope-intercept form.

Answer: y = −2x + 7

Parallel or Perpendicular?
Tests whether two lines (given their slopes) are parallel, perpendicular, or neither.
Inputs: m1= 3 m2= -1/3
Practice Question

Line a has slope 4. Line b has slope −1/4. Are they parallel, perpendicular, or neither?

Answer: Perpendicular (4 × −1/4 = −1)

Find Parallel/Perpendicular Lines
Given a slope and a point, finds the equations of the parallel and perpendicular lines through that point.
Inputs: m= 2 x1= 1 y1= 3
Practice Question

Find the equation of the line perpendicular to y = 3x + 1 that passes through (6, 2).

Enter: m= 3 x1= 6 y1= 2

Answer: y = −1/3 x + 4

📖 Reference Cards in Algebra
PEMDAS — Order of operations reminder
Direct Variation (y = kx) — k is the constant, line through origin
Linear Functions — y = mx + b, point-slope, standard form, parallel/perp rules
Absolute Value — |x| definition, y = a|x − h| + k vertex form
Intervals — (a,b), [a,b], half-open, infinity notation

2. Advanced Math (35% of SAT Math)

Quadratics, polynomials, rational/radical expressions, exponents, and logarithms.

Quadratic Equation (ax² + bx + c = 0)
Applies the quadratic formula. Shows discriminant, solutions (real or complex), and vertex.
Inputs: a= 1 b= -5 c= 6
Practice Question

What are the solutions to x² − 7x + 12 = 0?

Enter: a=1 b=-7 c=12

Answer: x = 3, x = 4

Complete the Square
Converts ax² + bx + c to vertex form a(x − h)² + k.
Inputs: a= 1 b= 6 c= 5
Practice Question

Rewrite x² + 8x + 12 in vertex form.

Enter: a=1 b=8 c=12

Answer: (x + 4)² − 4, vertex at (−4, −4)

Complete Sq → Vertex
Finds vertex, axis of symmetry, and direction of opening from a, b, c.
Inputs: a= -2 b= 8 c= -3
Practice Question

The function f(x) = −x² + 6x − 5 has a maximum value. What is it?

Enter: a=-1 b=6 c=-5

Answer: Maximum = 4 at vertex (3, 4)

Polynomial Explorer
Finds degree, zeros, factored form, derivative, and coefficients of a polynomial.
Input: p(x)= x^3-6x^2+11x-6
Practice Question

How many real zeros does p(x) = x³ − 4x have?

Enter: x^3-4x

Answer: 3 zeros: x = −2, 0, 2

Rational Expression Simplifier
Factors numerator and denominator, simplifies, and shows domain restrictions.
Inputs: Numerator= x^2-4 Denominator= x+2
Practice Question

Simplify: (x² − 9) / (x² − x − 6)

Enter: Num = x^2-9, Den = x^2-x-6

Answer: (x + 3)/(x + 2), x ≠ 3, x ≠ −2

Radical Simplifier
Simplifies radical expressions and provides exact and decimal results.
Input: Expression= sqrt(72)
Practice Question

Simplify √200 + √50.

Enter: sqrt(200)+sqrt(50)

Answer: 15√2 ≈ 21.213

Factor Integers
Prime factorization, all divisors, divisor pairs, and primality test.
Input: Integer= 360
Practice Question

How many positive divisors does 120 have?

Enter: 120

Answer: 16 divisors. 120 = 2³ × 3 × 5

Powers
Computes b^e, its inverse, and the n-th root.
Inputs: Base= 5 Exponent= 3
Practice Question

If 2^x = 64, what is x?

Enter: Base=2 Exponent=6 → see that 2⁶ = 64

Answer: x = 6

GCD & LCM
Finds GCD and LCM with Euclidean algorithm steps and prime factorizations.
Inputs: Integer1= 24 Integer2= 36
Practice Question

Two runners lap a track every 12 and 18 minutes. When do they next meet at start?

Enter: 12 and 18

Answer: LCM(12, 18) = 36 minutes

Solve Exponential/Log Equation
Solves equations involving exponents or logarithms for x.
Input: Equation= 3^x=81
Practice Question

Solve: 5^(2x) = 125

Enter: 5^(2x)=125

Answer: x = 3/2

Rule of 72
Estimates how long it takes an investment to double at a given interest rate.
Input: Rate(%)= 8
Practice Question

An account earns 6% annual interest. Approximately how many years to double?

Answer: 72 ÷ 6 = 12 years

Exponential Growth
Computes P(t) = P₀(1 + r)^t and continuous growth P₀e^(rt).
Inputs: P0= 1000 rate(%)= 5 t= 10
Practice Question

A population of 500 bacteria grows 20% per hour. How many after 5 hours?

Enter: P0=500 rate=20 t=5

Answer: ≈ 1,244

Evaluate Logarithm
Computes log_b(value) for any base.
Inputs: Base= 2 Value= 32
Practice Question

What is log₃(81)?

Enter: Base=3 Value=81

Answer: 4 (since 3⁴ = 81)

Change of Base
Converts log_b(x) to ln(x)/ln(b).
Inputs: Base= 5 Value= 30
Practice Question

Express log₇(50) as a ratio of natural logarithms and evaluate.

Answer: ln(50)/ln(7) ≈ 2.012

📖 Reference Cards in Advanced Math
Exponent Rules — x^a · x^b = x^(a+b), (x^a)^b = x^(ab), x⁰ = 1, x^(-a) = 1/x^a
Log Rules — log(xy) = log x + log y, log(x^n) = n·log x, change of base

3. Functions

Function Explorer
Finds zeros, y-intercept, symmetry (even/odd), critical points, min/max, derivative, and integral.
Input: f(x)= x^2-4x+3
Practice Question

The function f(x) = x² − 6x + 8 has how many x-intercepts?

Enter: x^2-6x+8

Answer: 2 zeros at x = 2 and x = 4

Evaluate Function
Plugs a value into f(x) to compute f(a).
Inputs: f(x)= 3x^2-2x+1 x= 4
Practice Question

If f(x) = 2x³ − x + 5, what is f(−2)?

Enter: f(x)=2x^3-x+5 x=-2

Answer: f(−2) = −9

Intersection of Two Functions
Finds where f(x) = g(x) — both exact and numeric solutions.
Inputs: f(x)= x^2 g(x)= 2x+3
Practice Question

At what point(s) does y = x² intersect y = x + 6?

Enter: f(x)=x^2 g(x)=x+6

Answer: x = 3 (y = 9) and x = −2 (y = 4)

Function Arithmetic (f+g, f−g, fg, f/g)
Computes combined function operations.
Inputs: f(x)= x^2+1 g(x)= x-3
Practice Question

If f(x) = x + 2 and g(x) = x², what is (f · g)(3)?

Answer: (f·g)(x) = x³ + 2x², so (f·g)(3) = 27 + 18 = 45

Composition f(g(x))
Computes f(g(x)) and g(f(x)).
Inputs: f(x)= x^2 g(x)= x+3
Practice Question

If f(x) = 2x + 1 and g(x) = x², what is f(g(3))?

Enter: f(x)=2x+1 g(x)=x^2

Answer: g(3) = 9, f(9) = 19

Complex Numbers (1 or 2)
Real/imaginary parts, modulus, angle, conjugate. For 2 numbers: +, −, ×, ÷.
Input: z1= 3+4i
Practice Question

What is (2 + 3i)(4 − i)?

Enter two complex: z1=2+3i z2=4-i

Answer: 11 + 10i

4. Problem Solving & Data Analysis (15% of SAT Math)

Ratios, percentages, statistics, probability, and data interpretation.

Solve Proportion
Cross-multiplication: a/b = c/d. Leave one blank to solve.
Inputs: a=3 b=4 c=9 d= (leave blank)
Practice Question

If 5/8 = x/24, what is x?

Enter: a=5 b=8 c= (blank) d=24

Answer: x = 15

Percent Change
Calculates (New − Old)/|Old| × 100 with step-by-step work and multiplier.
Inputs: Old Value= 80 New Value= 100
Practice Question

A shirt originally costs $45 and is now $36. What is the percent decrease?

Enter: Old=45 New=36

Answer: 20% decrease

Mean, Median, Mode
Computes central tendency measures from a comma-separated data list.
Input: Data(csv)= 5,8,3,7,9,3,6
Practice Question

The test scores are: 72, 85, 91, 68, 85, 77, 90. What is the median?

Enter: 72,85,91,68,85,77,90

Answer: Median = 85

Standard Deviation
Population σ, sample s, and variance from comma-separated data.
Input: Data(csv)= 4,8,6,5,3,7,9
Practice Question

Data set: 10, 12, 14, 16, 18. What is the population standard deviation?

Enter: 10,12,14,16,18

Answer: σ ≈ 2.828

Five-Number Summary
Min, Q1, Median, Q3, Max, and IQR.
Input: Data(csv)= 2,4,6,8,10,12,14
Practice Question

Data: 3, 5, 7, 9, 11, 13, 15, 17. What is the IQR?

Enter: 3,5,7,9,11,13,15,17

Answer: Q1 = 6, Q3 = 14, IQR = 8

Linear Regression
Fits y = mx + b to paired data. Shows slope, intercept, and R².
Inputs: x vals= 1,2,3,4,5 y vals= 2,4,5,4,5
Practice Question

Hours studied: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5. Scores: 50, 55, 65, 70, 80. Find the best-fit line.

Enter: x = 1,2,3,4,5 y = 50,55,65,70,80

Answer: y = 7x + 43, R² ≈ 0.982

Two-Way Table
Builds a 2×2 table and computes joint, marginal, and conditional probabilities.
Inputs: a(R1C1)=30 b(R1C2)=20 c(R2C1)=10 d(R2C2)=40
Practice Question

In a survey, 45 students like math, 25 don't. Of those who like math, 30 are in honors. Of those who don't, 5 are in honors. What is P(Honors | Likes Math)?

Enter: a=30 b=15 c=5 d=20

Answer: P(Honors|Likes Math) = 30/45 = 0.667

Margin of Error
Computes margin of error and confidence interval for a proportion.
Inputs: p= 0.6 n= 200 Conf Level%= 95
Practice Question

In a poll of 400 voters, 55% support a candidate. What is the 95% confidence interval?

Enter: p=0.55 n=400 Conf=95

Answer: MOE ≈ 0.0488, CI: (50.1%, 59.9%)

Basic Probability
P = favorable/total, odds for, odds against, complement.
Inputs: Favorable= 3 Total= 10
Practice Question

A bag has 4 red, 6 blue, and 5 green marbles. What is P(blue)?

Enter: Favorable=6 Total=15

Answer: P = 6/15 = 0.4 = 40%

nCr Combinations / nPr Permutations
C(n,r) for unordered selections; P(n,r) for ordered arrangements.
Inputs: n= 10 r= 3
Practice Question

A committee of 3 is chosen from 8 people. How many ways?

Enter: n=8 r=3

Answer: C(8,3) = 56

5. Geometry & Trigonometry (15% of SAT Math)

Areas, volumes, circles, triangles, coordinate geometry, and right-triangle trig.

Circle
Area, circumference, and diameter from radius.
Input: r= 7
Practice Question

A circular garden has radius 10 feet. What is its area?

Answer: A = π(10²) = 100π ≈ 314.159 ft²

Circle Equation
Converts between standard form (x−h)² + (y−k)² = r² and general form. Shows center, radius, area.
Inputs: h= 3 k= -2 r= 5
Practice Question

A circle has center (−1, 4) and radius 3. What is the equation in standard form?

Enter: h=-1 k=4 r=3

Answer: (x + 1)² + (y − 4)² = 9

Pythagorean Theorem
Computes c = √(a² + b²).
Inputs: a= 5 b= 12
Practice Question

A right triangle has legs 8 and 15. What is the hypotenuse?

Answer: c = √(64 + 225) = √289 = 17

Triangle / Rectangle / Square / Parallelogram / Trapezoid
Area, perimeter, and diagonal for each shape.
Practice Question

A trapezoid has parallel sides 6 cm and 10 cm, height 4 cm. What is the area?

Use Trapezoid: a=6 b=10 h=4

Answer: A = ½(6 + 10)(4) = 32 cm²

Sphere / Cube / Cylinder / Cone / Pyramid / Rectangular Prism
Volume, surface area, and related measurements for 3D shapes.
Practice Question

A cone has radius 4 and height 9. What is its volume?

Use Cone: r=4 h=9

Answer: V = ⅓π(16)(9) = 48π ≈ 150.796

Pyramid Volume
V = (1/3)lwh — directly from the SAT reference sheet.
Inputs: l= 6 w= 4 h= 9
Practice Question

A rectangular pyramid has a base 8 × 5 and height 12. What is the volume?

Enter: l=8 w=5 h=12

Answer: V = (1/3)(8)(5)(12) = 160

Sector Area & Arc Length
Area = ½r²θ and arc length = rθ (angle in degrees, converted internally).
Inputs: r= 10 theta= 60
Practice Question

A pizza slice from a 14-inch diameter pizza has a 45° central angle. What is the area of the slice?

Use Sector: r=7 theta=45

Answer: A = ½(49)(π/4) ≈ 19.242 in²

Distance Formula / Midpoint Formula
d = √((x₂−x₁)² + (y₂−y₁)²) and midpoint = ((x₁+x₂)/2, (y₁+y₂)/2).
Inputs: x1=1 y1=2 x2=7 y2=10
Practice Question

What is the distance between (−3, 4) and (5, −2)?

Enter: x1=-3 y1=4 x2=5 y2=-2

Answer: d = √(64 + 36) = √100 = 10

Solve Right Triangle
Enter any 2 of 3 sides (a, b, hypotenuse c). Finds missing side, angles, area, perimeter.
Inputs: a= 5 b= (blank) c= 13
Practice Question

A ladder leans against a wall. Base is 6 ft from the wall, ladder is 10 ft. How high up the wall?

Enter: a=6 b= (blank) c=10

Answer: b = 8 ft

Evaluate sin/cos/tan
Computes sin, cos, and tan for any angle in degrees.
Input: x (deg)= 30
Practice Question

What is the value of sin(45°)?

Enter: 45

Answer: sin(45°) = √2/2 ≈ 0.707

Degree ↔ Radian
Converts between degrees and radians.
Input: Degrees= 180
Practice Question

Convert 270° to radians.

Answer: 270° = 3π/2 ≈ 4.712 rad

6. SAT Specials

Sigma Notation
Computes Σ f(n) from n = a to n = b.
Inputs: f(n)= n^2 from n= 1 to n= 10
Practice Question

What is the sum of 2n + 1 for n = 1 to 5?

Enter: f(n)=2n+1 from=1 to=5

Answer: 3 + 5 + 7 + 9 + 11 = 35

Count Integers / Count Divisible
Counts integers in a range, optionally divisible by N and/or M (uses inclusion-exclusion).
Count: Low=1 High=100   Divisible: + N=3 M=5
Practice Question

How many integers from 1 to 200 are divisible by both 4 and 6?

Use Count Divisible: Low=1 High=200 N=4 M=6

Answer: LCM(4,6) = 12. Count = 16 integers divisible by both.

Integer Solutions: 2 or 3 Variables
Solves equations in multiple variables using CAS.
Input: Equation= x+y=10
Practice Question

Find all positive integer solutions to 3x + 2y = 20.

Answer: (2,7), (4,4), (6,1)
